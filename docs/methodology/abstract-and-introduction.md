# Bridge-Manifold-Interface (BMI) Theory: A Unified Framework

## Abstract
[cite_start]This paper introduces the Bridge-Manifold-Interface (BMI) theory, a modified gravity framework proposing that our 4D universe exists as a 'Thick Brane' interface within a multi-manifold 11D bulk[cite: 3]. [cite_start]The BMI model posits that apparent anomalies of Lambda-CDM—specifically Dark Energy, the Axis of Evil, and galactic rotation curves—are emergent consequences of the interaction between our manifold and an adjacent twin universe[cite: 4]. 

[cite_start]By introducing a density-dependent screening gate function, the theory reconciles large-scale hyperspatial shear with high-precision local gravity experiments[cite: 5]. [cite_start]Empirical validation was conducted using high-energy datasets from the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), specifically analyzing monojet event distributions and Missing Transverse Energy (MET) spectra at 13 TeV[cite: 6, 7]. [cite_start]The model constrains the interaction coupling constant ($\alpha$) to values consistent with the absence of resonant graviton production below the 5 TeV scale, suggesting a geometric origin for dark sector phenomena that bypasses the need for undiscovered particle species[cite: 7, 8].

## 1. Introduction
[cite_start]The current standard cosmological model (Lambda-CDM) relies on three unobserved pillars: Dark Matter, Dark Energy, and the assumption of large-scale statistical isotropy[cite: 10]. [cite_start]While successful, these pillars remain empirically undetectable as distinct particles or fields[cite: 11]. 

[cite_start]The BMI theory emerges from the observation that the density of the observable universe is equivalent to a black hole of its own Schwarzschild radius, suggesting that our universe may be engaged in a 'frustrated merger' with an adjacent manifold[cite: 12]. [cite_start]This framework defines the interface ($\Sigma$) as a thick brane that confines gauge fields while allowing gravitational coupling[cite: 13]. 

[cite_start]The model demonstrates that this geometry naturally leads to[cite: 14]:
1. **Dynamical Dark Energy**: Modeled as a thermodynamic entropy tax.
2. **Galactic Rotation Curves**: Emergent as a consequence of tidal shadow potential.
3. **Alignment Anomalies**: Interpreted as a signature of hyperspatial shear.

[cite_start]By grounding the theory in 11D supergravity and testing it against LHC monojet constraints, we present a falsifiable and mathematically self-consistent alternative to standard dark-sector paradigms[cite: 15].
