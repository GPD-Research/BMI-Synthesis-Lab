## 3.1 Restoration of the Bianchi Identity
To ensure a consistent theory of gravity, the modified field equations ($G^{\mu\nu} + \alpha H^{\mu\nu} = \kappa^2 T^{\mu\nu}$) must satisfy the local conservation of energy-momentum, $\nabla_{\mu} T^{\mu\nu} = 0$. [cite_start]Since the Einstein tensor $G^{\mu\nu}$ satisfies the Bianchi Identity ($\nabla_{\mu} G^{\mu\nu} = 0$), local conservation requires $\nabla_{\mu} H^{\mu\nu} = 0$[cite: 3]. [cite_start]In high-density regimes where $g_{eff}(R) \to 0$, the interaction tensor $H^{\mu\nu}$ vanishes, ensuring recovery of General Relativity[cite: 3].

## 3.2 Graviton Trapping and Bulk Suppression
[cite_start]To preserve microscopic causality and Lorentz invariance, bulk gravitons are effectively 'trapped' by the screening term $g_{eff}(R)$[cite: 3]. As matter density increases, $g_{eff}(R)$ drops significantly (reaching $\approx 10^{-36}$), effectively renormalizing the coupling constant:
$$G_{eff} = G_N \cdot g_{eff}(R)$$
[cite_start]This renormalization creates a Dirichlet-like boundary condition, confining the gravitational degree of freedom to the 4D null cone[cite: 3].

## 3.3 Evanescent Field Localization
[cite_start]To satisfy $U(1)$ gauge symmetry without allowing photons to cross the manifold interface, we employ evanescent localization within the Thick Brane ($\Sigma$)[cite: 3]. The wave equation is governed by the 5D Laplace-Beltrami operator:
$$(\Box_4 + \partial_y^2) A_{\mu}(x, y) = 0$$
Assuming a solution $A_{\mu}(x, y) = A_{\mu}(x) e^{-|m| [cite_start]|y|}$, the field amplitude decays exponentially with distance into the interface[cite: 3]. As the brane thickness approaches the bulk limit, the boundary condition becomes:
$$\lim_{y \to \Delta} A_{\mu}(x, y) = 0$$
[cite_start]This confirms that standard model gauge bosons are strictly confined, preventing 'dual citizenship' of gauge fields across the bridge[cite: 3].
