## 3.1 Restoration of the Bianchi Identity
To ensure a consistent theory of gravity, the modified field equations must satisfy the local conservation of energy-momentum, $\nabla_{\mu}T^{\mu\nu}=0$. Starting from our modified Einstein field equations:

$$G_{\mu\nu} + \alpha H_{\mu\nu} = \kappa^2 T_{\mu\nu}$$

Applying the covariant divergence operator $\nabla_{\mu}$ to both sides yields:

$$\nabla_{\mu}G_{\mu\nu} + \alpha \nabla_{\mu}H_{\mu\nu} = \kappa^2 \nabla_{\mu}T_{\mu\nu}$$

Since the Einstein tensor $G_{\mu\nu}$ automatically satisfies the Bianchi Identity $\nabla_{\mu}G_{\mu\nu}=0$, the condition for local energy-momentum conservation requires:

$$\nabla_{\mu}H_{\mu\nu} = 0$$

In the high-density limit, where $g_{\text{eff}}(R) \to 0$, the interaction tensor $H_{\mu\nu}$ effectively vanishes, ensuring the theory recovers General Relativity exactly. In lower-density regimes, the structure of $H_{\mu\nu}$ is constrained by the hyperspatial shear such that its divergence vanishes identically, preserving the equivalence principle.

## 3.2 Graviton Trapping and Bulk Suppression
Gravitational interaction across the manifold interface $\Sigma$ is mediated by the hyperspatial shear tensor $S_{\mu\nu}$. However, to preserve microscopic causality and Lorentz invariance in our manifold, the bulk gravitons must be effectively 'trapped'. The interaction action includes the screening term $g_{\text{eff}}(R)$. As the local matter density increases, $g_{\text{eff}}(R)$ drops to $10^{-36}$, creating a Dirichlet-like boundary condition for gravity. Mathematically, the effective coupling constant for bulk interactions is renormalized:

$$G_N = G_N \cdot g_{\text{eff}}(R)$$

This forces the kinetic term for higher-dimensional perturbations to zero in local regions, confining the gravitational degree of freedom strictly to the 4D null cone.
