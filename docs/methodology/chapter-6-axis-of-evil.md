## 6.1 The Geometrical Origin of Alignment
The 'Axis of Evil' refers to the anomalous alignment of the low-multipole moments (quadrupole and octupole) of the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)[cite: 8]. In the BMI framework, these alignments are interpreted as the projected signature of the tidal shear tensor $S_{\mu\nu}$ acting across the manifold interface ($\Sigma$)[cite: 8].

## 6.2 The Shear Tensor and CMB Multipoles
The hyperspatial shear tensor $S_{\mu\nu}$ creates persistent large-scale gravitational stress on our manifold[cite: 8]. This modifies the Sachs-Wolfe effect, inducing a perturbation field $\Delta T/T$ that inherits preferred directionality due to the relative tilt of the manifolds[cite: 8]. The power spectrum is defined as[cite: 8]:
$$P(k) = P_{iso}(k) \left[ 1 + \beta (k_i n^i)^2 \right]$$
Where $\beta$ represents the coupling strength, and $n^i$ is the normal vector to the hyperspatial bridge[cite: 8]. This naturally aligns the quadrupole ($l=2$) and octupole ($l=3$) moments along the axis defined by the bulk stress[cite: 8].

## 6.3 Interpretation of the Cold Spot
In the BMI framework, the CMB Cold Spot represents an 'interface rupture'—a localized region where the screening gate $g_{eff}(R)$ allows for enhanced hyperspatial tunneling[cite: 8]. This tunneling enables energy loss into the twin manifold, resulting in a temperature deficit calculated as[cite: 8]:
$$\Delta T_{loss} = \int \left[ \alpha \cdot g_{eff}(R) \cdot dV \right]$$
This maps the Cold Spot to specific geometric coordinates $(\theta, \phi)$ on the brane interface, acting as a diagnostic of the twin universe's proximity[cite: 8].
