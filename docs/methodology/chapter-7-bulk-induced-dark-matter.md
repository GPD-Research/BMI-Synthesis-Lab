# Chapter 7: Bulk-Induced Dark Matter Effects

## 7.1 Reinterpreting Missing Mass as Tidal Shadowing
[cite_start]In the BMI framework, the 'missing mass' problem is not caused by undetected particle matter (WIMPs)[cite: 57]. [cite_start]Instead, it is an effective gravitational ghost-effect[cite: 58]. [cite_start]The hyperspatial shear tensor $S_{\mu\nu}$, which acts as the 'coupling' between manifolds, exerts a persistent tidal pull on our local spacetime geometry[cite: 58]. [cite_start]We define the total gravitational potential $\Phi$ as the sum of local baryonic contributions and the induced shadow potential $\Phi_{\text{shadow}}$[cite: 59]:

$$\nabla^2 \Phi = 4\pi G(\rho_{\text{baryon}} + \rho_{\text{shadow}})$$

## 7.2 The Ghost Density Equation
[cite_start]The 'shadow density' $\rho_{\text{shadow}}$ is not a physical mass, but a projection of the adjacent manifold's mass distribution onto our brane via the hyperspatial bridge[cite: 62]. [cite_start]We derive this by projecting the bulk curvature through the interface thickness $\Sigma$[cite: 63]:

$$\rho_{\text{shadow}} = \beta \int S_{00} \sqrt{-g} dy$$

[cite_start]Where $\beta$ is the coupling constant calibrated by the local curvature gate $g_{\text{eff}}(R)$[cite: 65]. [cite_start]In high-density regions (like the solar system), $R \gg R_c$, making $g_{\text{eff}}(R) \to 0$[cite: 66]. [cite_start]Consequently, $\rho_{\text{shadow}}$ vanishes, explaining why we do not detect 'Dark Matter' in local solar system dynamics[cite: 67].

## 7.3 Flattening of Rotation Curves
[cite_start]For galactic scales, where the local density drops and the screening gate $g_{\text{eff}}(R)$ transitions to unity, the ghost potential becomes significant[cite: 69]. [cite_start]The velocity of a test particle at radius $r$ is governed by[cite: 70]:

$$v^2 = \frac{GM_{\text{baryon}}}{r} + \frac{GM_{\text{shadow}}}{r_{\text{eff}}}$$

[cite_start]As $r$ increases, the baryonic contribution $M_{\text{baryon}}/r$ plateaus, but the shadow contribution $M_{\text{shadow}}$ remains correlated to the hyperspatial bridge tilt[cite: 72]. [cite_start]This forces $v(r)$ toward a constant value, naturally producing the observed flat rotation curves without requiring dark matter haloes[cite: 73].

## 7.4 The Shadow Density-Mass Link
[cite_start]The ghost-effect potential $\Phi_{\text{shadow}}$ responsible for galactic rotation curves is coupled to the local particle mass spectrum[cite: 75]. [cite_start]Specifically, the neutrino mass $m_{\nu} = \phi_{\text{vev}} \cdot T_{\text{shear}} \cdot R_{\text{bridge}} \cdot V_{\text{shadow}}^{-1}$ suggests that the shadow manifold exerts a dual influence: macroscopic (rotation curves) and microscopic (neutrino mass)[cite: 76]. [cite_start]This confirms that shadow geometry is the primary regulator of gravitational and inertial mass[cite: 77].
