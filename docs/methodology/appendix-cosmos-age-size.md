# Appendix: On the Age and Size of the Cosmos

## 1. The Cosmic Age Parameter ($t_0$)
[cite_start]To ensure the BMI framework aligns with observational data, we verify the temporal age of the manifold[cite: 366, 368]. [cite_start]In regions where the screening gate transitions to its general relativistic limit ($g_{eff} \to 1$), the background Hubble parameter follows standard energy density evolution[cite: 371]. The age is calculated via the integral:
$$t_0 = \int_0^1 \frac{da}{a \cdot H(a)}$$
Using concordance values ($H_0 \approx 67.4$ km/s/Mpc, $\Omega_{m,0} \approx 0.315$, $\Omega_{\Lambda,0} \approx 0.685$), this yields:
$$t_0 = \frac{2}{3 H_0 \sqrt{\Omega_{\Lambda,0}}} \ln \left( \frac{\sqrt{\Omega_{\Lambda,0}} + 1}{\sqrt{\Omega_{m,0}}} \right) \approx 13.787 \times 10^9 \text{ years}$$
[cite_start]This temporal baseline remains sound and aligned with CMB data[cite: 377, 378, 379, 380, 381].

## 2. Spatial Scale and Volume of the Nested Torus ($T^3 \times T^3$)
[cite_start]The universe is modeled as a nested topological manifold, represented as flat Riemannian three-tori[cite: 383, 384].

### Manifold A: The Local Manifold ($M_{local}$)
* [cite_start]The fundamental scale $L_{local}$ must exceed the diameter of the observable causal sphere ($d_H \approx 46.5$ billion light-years) to avoid recurring topological ghost images[cite: 386, 388].
* [cite_start]Constraint: $L_{local} \gtrsim 93 \times 10^9$ light-years[cite: 389].
* [cite_start]Minimum Volume: $V_{local} \gtrsim 8.04 \times 10^{32}$ ly$^3$[cite: 393].

### Manifold B: The Compactified Shadow Manifold ($M_{shadow}$)
* [cite_start]The secondary manifold exists at a compactification scale $L_{shadow} \ll d_H$[cite: 395, 398].
* [cite_start]This secondary volume governs structural constraints for auxiliary fields like $\Phi_{shadow}$[cite: 396].

### Topological Volume Relations
[cite_start]The ratio of spatial extents determines the structural coupling gradient across cosmic voids[cite: 402]:
$$\frac{V_{local}}{V_{shadow}} = \left( \frac{L_{local}}{L_{shadow}} \right)^3$$
[cite_start]This model preserves a finite, un-bordered global volume that remains consistent with our observable horizon while facilitating the necessary hyperspatial coupling[cite: 404].
