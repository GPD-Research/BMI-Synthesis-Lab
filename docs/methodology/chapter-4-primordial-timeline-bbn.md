# Chapter 4: The Primordial Timeline and BBN Protection

## 4.1 Curvature Profile of the Radiation-Dominated Era
A central challenge for higher-dimensional cosmologies is avoiding the 'Hyperspatial Stiff Fluid' effect, where brane-bulk interactions introduce a density-squared ($\rho^2$) term into the Friedmann equation. In the BMI framework, the screening function $g_{eff}(R)$ behaves asymptotically as:
$$\lim_{R \to \infty} g_{eff}(R) = \lim_{R \to \infty} \left[ \frac{1}{1 + (R/R_c)^n} \right] = 0$$
This effectively deactivates the hyperspatial interface during the radiation-dominated era, shielding the 4D manifold from bulk-induced pressure.

## 4.2 Suppression of the Hyperspatial Stiff Fluid
The modified Friedmann equation in the BMI theory incorporates the shear tensor contribution through the coupling term $\alpha \cdot g_{eff}(R)$. The effective energy density on the brane is:
$$H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3} \left[ \rho + \alpha \cdot g_{eff}(R) \cdot \rho^2 \right]$$
Because $g_{eff}(R) \to 0$ for high energy densities, the quadratic $\rho^2$ term is suppressed by the inverse-power gate function. During Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN), the interaction is attenuated to the order of $O(10^{-20})$, rendering the bulk contribution negligible.

## 4.3 Preserving Light Element Abundances
BBN is highly sensitive to the expansion rate $H(t)$. Since the BMI expansion rate $H(t)_{BMI}$ remains effectively identical to the standard expansion history $H(t)_{standard}$ for temperatures $T > 0.1$ MeV, the theory is fully consistent with observed primordial abundances, such as Helium-4 ($Y_p$) and Deuterium (D/H). This confirms that the BMI framework introduces no anomalous relativistic degrees of freedom that would threaten standard BBN predictions.
