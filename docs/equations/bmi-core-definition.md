# Core Definition: Bimodal Manifold Interaction (BMI)

## 1. Primary Hypothesis
The Bimodal Manifold Interaction (BMI) model postulates that cosmological dynamics are driven by the periodic and recursive exchange of energy-information states between two distinct yet coupled topological manifolds, denoted as $M_1$ and $M_2$. We hypothesize that the observed expansion of the universe is not an intrinsic property of a singular spacetime, but an emergent effect of the non-linear coupling interaction between these bimodal manifolds.

## 2. Interaction Mechanics
The interaction is governed by the state vector $\Psi$, which represents the transition probability and energy flux across the shared boundary $\partial M$.

### 2.1 The Manifold Coupling Equation
The coupling interaction is defined by the exchange operator $\hat{I}$, acting upon the manifold states:

$$\hat{I}(\Psi_1, \Psi_2) = \oint_{\partial M} (\nabla \phi_1 \cdot \nabla \phi_2) \, dA + \mathcal{K}(\tau)$$

Where:
* $\phi_1, \phi_2$ are the scalar field potentials of manifolds $M_1$ and $M_2$.
* $\mathcal{K}(\tau)$ is the time-dependent coupling constant regulating the frequency of interaction.
* $\partial M$ represents the intersection boundary (the "manifold interface").

### 2.2 Energy-Information Conservation
The conservation of total manifold energy $E_{tot}$ within the bimodal system requires:

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial t} \int_{M_1 \cup M_2} \rho \, dV = -\int_{\partial M} \vec{J}_{BMI} \cdot d\vec{S}$$

Where:
* $\rho$ is the energy density distribution.
* $\vec{J}_{BMI}$ represents the flux density of the BMI interaction across the interface.

## 3. Theoretical Constraints
For the BMI model to be consistent with observed cosmological data, the following constraints must hold:
1.  **Symmetry Breaking:** The interaction must allow for a spontaneous symmetry breaking between $\phi_1$ and $\phi_2$ to account for baryonic matter distribution.
2.  **Manifold Topology:** $M_1$ and $M_2$ are non-orientable in local regions, facilitating the "bimodal" flip observed as particle-antiparticle asymmetry.
