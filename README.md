# BMI-Synthesis-Lab: A Collaborative Framework for Bimodal Manifold Interaction and AI-Research Fidelity Analysis

## Project Overview
Welcome to the **BMI-Synthesis-Lab**. This repository serves as the centralized research hub and version-controlled archive for the **Bimodal Manifold Interaction (BMI)** cosmological model. 

This project is unique in its execution: it operates as an active, documented collaboration between human domain expertise and Advanced Artificial Intelligence (Large Language Models). The repository is structured to advance the underlying physics of the BMI model while simultaneously evaluating the validity, boundaries, and fidelity of generative AI as an accelerator for theoretical scientific discovery.

---

## Dual Research Objectives

### 1. The Core Scientific Model: Bimodal Manifold Interaction (BMI)
The primary physics objective is to collate, mathematically formalize, and validate the BMI cosmological model. The research investigates higher-dimensional manifold dynamics, bulk space interactions, and potential explanations for cosmic anomalies.
* **Validation Phase:** Moving from conceptual frameworks to rigorous symbolic testing and quantitative verification.
* **Software Integration:** Utilizing scientific mathematics validation software and computational physics tools to test the boundaries of the model's equations.

### 2. The Meta-Analysis: AI-Research Fidelity Evaluation
The secondary methodological objective is to assess the utility of LLM AIs as legitimate partners in complex theoretical development. 
* **Fidelity Testing:** Tracking the accuracy, deductive capabilities, and consistency of AI interactions when dealing with novel, highly specialized theoretical data.
* **Process Transparency:** Documenting the prompt-to-output pipeline to evaluate the AI's ability to maintain context, avoid hallucination, and handle complex symbolic logic over long iterations.
